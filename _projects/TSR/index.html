Deep High-Resolution Representation Learning (HRNet)

Introduction

Recent works in multiple object tracking use sequence model to calculate the similarity score between the detections and the previous tracklets. However, matching-based sequence model formulation, training-inference discrepancy, i.e., exposure bias and improper optimization objective impair the overall performance. In this paper, we propose a novel framework to evaluate tracklets' quality, which is a common approach extensible to different association methods. It offers an online, end-to-end tracklet search process to reduce the training-inference discrepancy. To increase the discrimination capability of the model, it designs a composite loss of contrast learning to punish the undesirable tracklets with ID switch. Our tracker achieves remarkable performance gain from conventional tracklet association baseline. Our methods have also achieved state-of-the-art on MOT15~17 challenge benchmarks using public detection and online settings.
      We propose a high-resolution network (HRNet). The HRNet maintains high-resolution representations by connecting high-to-low resolution convolutions in parallel and strengthens high-resolution representations by repeatedly performing multi-scale fusions across parallel convolutions. We demonstrate the effectives on pixel-level classification, region-level classification, and image-level classification.
      The HRNet turns out to be a strong repalcement of classification networks (e.g., ResNets, VGGNets) for visual recognition. We believe that the HRNet will become the new standard backbone.
